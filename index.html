The Opportunity

The Dominican Republic is Brazil circa 2013. Nubank saw it first — NICO is doing it better.

01

The Parallel Is Undeniable

When Nubank launched in Brazil, 60% of adults were unbanked, mobile penetration was surging, and legacy banks held a stranglehold through fees and friction. The Dominican Republic sits at that exact inflection point today — with 11M people, $12B in remittances, and zero credible digital challenger.

02

The Underserved Majority

Over 60% of DR adults have no meaningful banking access. They operate in cash, accumulate no credit history, and have no structured path to financial security. This is not a niche — it is the entire mass market, waiting to be activated by the right product.

03

NICO's Structural Advantage

NICO is not a bank that added technology. It is an AI system designed from the infrastructure layer to serve this market — with a remittance-first distribution strategy that turns organic money flows into near-zero-cost customer acquisition at scale.

Product Suite

Four products. One platform. A complete financial life for people who've never had one.

🔄

Automatic Round-Ups

Every transaction rounds up — spare change flows silently into dedicated sub-accounts. Users build savings without effort. Each Round-Up is a micro-investment in engagement and loyalty. Stickiness by design, not by lock-in.

🎓

NICO Fintech Bites

Bite-sized financial education woven into the daily app experience — non-invasive, personalised, and delivered without lecturing. Financial literacy as a product feature creates retention that no competitor can buy. Users who learn stay, cross-sell, and refer.

💳

NICO Cards

Virtual and physical debit cards issued on account approval. Real-time spend visibility, instant notifications, zero foreign transaction friction. First financial product millions of these users will ever own — NICO owns that relationship from day one.

🤲

Empathetic Credit (BNPL)

AI-powered BNPL triggered by behavioural insight, not credit bureau scores. NICO can underwrite customers traditional lenders cannot see — unlocking a massive, high-margin credit book in a market with almost no existing consumer credit penetration.
